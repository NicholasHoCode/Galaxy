Introduction (Last Updated: May 2017)

I received my B.Sc. in Computer Science & Statistics at the University of British Columbia Vancouver in 2017. My interests are in mathematics, statistical modeling, software development, machine learning, computer vision & scientific computing. When I write code I usually do it in Python, R and MATLAB, but historically I’ve used Java (hence, a good basic understanding of C#) and a fair amount of C/C++ and Javascript. Most of what I’ve been doing last few years has been machine learning and back-end development (modeling database and designing RESTful services). In my spare time, I enjoy solving Project Euler problems, hacking on large datasets (terabytes of data), implementing interesting ideas in research papers from scratch and designing (and back-testing) my trading algorithms.
